## Sacramento Source Policy

### Allowed Channels
1. Site-operator introductions (lawful path into private interiors, requires named operator approval and rights packet)
2. Buyer-linked exact-site requests (strongest proof-led capture path, requires buyer thread plus operator approval)
3. Local surveying/AEC/industrial inspection firms (curated only, requires professional credentials plus first-capture review)
4. High-trust mapper referrals (held until proof assets exist, requires referral guardrails plus completion history)
5. Bounded online community sourcing for public, non-controlled commercial sites (groceries, retail, walk-in locations; requires public-area-only brief and privacy/signage guardrails)

### Disallowed Channels
- Public bounty programs
- Generic gig-worker sourcing
- Broad community sourcing for private controlled interiors
- Any channel implying private-interior access for public commercial lane participants

### Referral Rules
- High-trust mapper referrals are held until 3-5 proof-ready sites exist and first proof assets are cleared
- Referrals require completion history and adherence to public-area-only capture rules for public commercial lanes
- All referrals must route through Growth Lead for approval before invite/access-code issuance

### Invite/Access-Code Issuance Authority
- Routine invite and access-code decisions are owned by Growth Lead and Ops Lead inside written guardrails
- Private controlled interior access codes require verified site-operator authorization and Ops Lead sign-off
- Public commercial lane access codes require completion of public-area-only brief and privacy/signage acknowledgment
- No agent or automated system may issue invites/access codes without explicit Growth Lead or Ops Lead approval

### Public Commercial Lane Online Habitats
Everyday capturers for public, non-controlled commercial sites are sourced from:
- Local city/community groups
- Neighborhood forums
- Retail and shopping communities
- Creator communities
- Lightweight campus or gig networks that do not imply private-interior access

### Private Controlled Interiors Policy
- All private controlled interior capture requires explicit site-operator authorization and Ops Lead-approved intake rubric
- No public bounty, gig-worker, or broad community sourcing is permitted for private interiors
- Access to private interiors is restricted to site-authorized surveying, AEC, industrial inspection, or commercial mapping operators
