# New-Hire Paperwork Checklist

Status: Draft, requires counsel/PEO/payroll review before operational use.
Owner: Hiring manager with Finance Support Agent and HR/PEO.
Scope: U.S. employee onboarding paperwork and startup compliance setup.
Review cadence: Before each hiring wave, after law/vendor changes, and when hiring in a new state.

## Purpose

This checklist helps Blueprint onboard humans without losing legal, payroll, security, or source-of-truth discipline. It should be adapted by counsel/PEO for each employee location and classification.

## Before Offer Acceptance

- Approved role scorecard and budget.
- Employment classification reviewed: employee, contractor, intern, advisor, or vendor.
- Work location reviewed for payroll/tax/leave/workers' compensation/new-hire reporting.
- Compensation, equity, and start date approved by founder/CEO.
- Offer letter prepared through counsel/PEO/payroll path.
- Role-specific confidentiality/IP packet selected by counsel.

## After Offer Acceptance

- Signed offer letter.
- Signed confidentiality/IP agreement after counsel review.
- Handbook/policy acknowledgment after counsel/PEO approves the packet.
- Emergency contact process prepared if used by HR/PEO.
- Payroll profile created.
- Benefits eligibility packet prepared from active plan docs.
- Access and equipment checklist opened.
- Manager onboarding checklist opened.

## Federal Employment Eligibility

Use current USCIS Form I-9 instructions and employer guidance.

Operating notes:

- Employee completes Section 1 no later than first day of employment, consistent with USCIS rules.
- Employer or authorized representative completes Section 2 within the required period.
- Store I-9 records in the HR/PEO-controlled record system, not in Notion, Slack, or ordinary repo docs.
- Use current USCIS form/version and document-retention guidance.

Source: USCIS Form I-9 and Handbook for Employers resources at https://www.uscis.gov/i-9.

## Tax Withholding

- Federal Form W-4 completed through payroll/HR system.
- State withholding form completed when required by the work state.
- Employee tax questions should be routed to payroll/PEO/tax advisor, not guessed by managers.

Source: IRS Form W-4 resources at https://www.irs.gov/forms-pubs/about-form-w-4.

## New-Hire Reporting

Federal law requires employers to report basic new-hire or rehire information to the state where the employee works within the required window, and some states require faster reporting or extra fields.

Operating notes:

- Payroll/PEO should own submission where configured.
- Multistate reporting requires following ACF/OCSE multistate employer rules if Blueprint elects a single-state path.
- Record confirmation in the HR/payroll system or restricted compliance tracker.

Source: ACF/OCSE new-hire reporting guidance and state new-hire directories at https://acf.gov/css/employers/employer-responsibilities/new-hire-reporting.

## State And Local Setup

Counsel/PEO/payroll must verify:

- state income tax and withholding;
- unemployment insurance;
- workers' compensation;
- wage notice requirements;
- leave, sick time, final pay, and paid family leave;
- remote employee notice/poster requirements;
- E-Verify requirements where applicable;
- state-specific handbook requirements.

## Workplace Notices

Required workplace notices vary by law, employer size, work state, and remote/in-person posture. Use DOL Poster Advisor and state labor agency guidance. Remote employees may need electronic notice access depending on the jurisdiction.

Source: U.S. Department of Labor workplace poster guidance at https://www.dol.gov/general/topics/posters/.

## Systems And Access

Follow `docs/company/access-and-equipment-policy.md`.

Minimum access packet:

- company email/calendar;
- Slack/chat;
- Notion;
- GitHub/repo access where role requires it;
- Paperclip visibility or execution access where role requires it;
- payroll/benefits self-service;
- role-specific vendor systems after procurement/security review.

## First-Day Acknowledgments

- Handbook acknowledgment.
- Security and acceptable-use acknowledgment.
- Access and equipment acknowledgment.
- Confidentiality/IP agreement receipt.
- Remote-work policy acknowledgment.
- Code of conduct acknowledgment.

## Evidence Paths

- Signed employment docs: HR/PEO/counsel-controlled system.
- Access checklist: restricted tracker or Paperclip issue.
- Onboarding work: `docs/onboarding/manager-onboarding-checklist.md`.
- Policy packet: `docs/company/`.
- Notion visibility: Company Handbook & Onboarding surface after repo docs are mirrored.

## Legal Review Gaps

- Counsel/PEO must confirm employment classification, offer-letter terms, state withholding, state new-hire reporting, workers' compensation, unemployment, leave rules, wage notices, workplace notices, I-9 remote examination posture, E-Verify applicability, and document retention before the checklist is used for a live hire.
