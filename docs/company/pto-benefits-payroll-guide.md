# PTO, Benefits, And Payroll Guide

Status: Draft, requires counsel/PEO/payroll review. This guide does not create benefit entitlements until adopted in signed or plan documents.
Owner: Finance Support Agent with Founder/CEO and HR/PEO.
Scope: Employee payroll setup, tax withholding, benefits orientation, PTO/time-off process, and live-source gaps.
Review cadence: Before every hiring wave, when benefits/payroll vendors change, and quarterly.

## Purpose

This guide gives managers and new hires a conservative operating path for payroll, benefits, and time off while preventing unsupported promises.

## Source Of Truth

- Signed offer letter: compensation, role, start date, equity if any, and employment terms.
- Payroll/PEO system: payroll profile, tax withholding, direct deposit, pay statements.
- Benefits plan documents: eligibility, coverage, waiting periods, costs, and enrollment deadlines.
- Applicable law: wage, tax, leave, final pay, workers' compensation, unemployment, and state/local rules.
- Repo/Notion docs: orientation and review only.

If a benefit is not in a signed offer, approved plan document, or PEO/payroll system, treat it as source-needed.

## Payroll Setup

Before start date:

1. Confirm legal name, work location, start date, employment classification, manager, and payroll cadence through HR/PEO/payroll.
2. Collect federal Form W-4 and any required state withholding forms.
3. Collect direct deposit details through the payroll system, not through chat or Notion.
4. Complete I-9 workflow as required by USCIS rules.
5. Submit state new-hire reporting through the proper state or PEO/payroll process.
6. Confirm worker's compensation and unemployment setup for the employee's work state.

## Benefits

Benefits must be described from plan documents, not manager memory. The onboarding packet should link the active plan docs and identify:

- eligibility date;
- enrollment deadline;
- covered benefit types;
- employee cost;
- dependent rules;
- how to waive coverage;
- who to contact for benefit questions.

Current live-source status for this draft: source-needed until Blueprint has counsel/PEO-confirmed plan documents linked in the human-facing onboarding surface.

## PTO And Time Off

Until counsel/PEO adopts a formal plan, managers should use an approval-based time-off process and avoid promising unlimited, accrued, paid, unpaid, sick, parental, bereavement, jury-duty, or statutory leave terms beyond approved documents and applicable law.

Minimum operating process:

1. Employee requests time off through the approved calendar/HR system.
2. Manager reviews coverage, customer commitments, field work, and deadlines.
3. HR/PEO confirms any protected or statutory leave requirements.
4. Approved time is recorded in the payroll/HR system.
5. Critical handoffs are documented in Paperclip or the relevant repo/workspace.

## Pay Changes, Bonuses, And Equity

Only the founder/CEO or authorized finance owner may approve compensation changes, bonuses, commissions, or equity terms. Equity and incentive compensation require counsel-reviewed documentation.

## Final Pay And Separation

Final pay, unused time-off treatment, benefits continuation, and separation paperwork vary by location and must be handled through HR/PEO/counsel. Follow `docs/company/offboarding-checklist.md`.

## Employee Questions

Employees should ask payroll/benefits questions through the designated HR/PEO/payroll channel once configured. Managers should not guess on tax, immigration, benefits, or leave matters.
