# Confidentiality And IP Assignment Packet

Status: Draft, requires counsel/PEO review. This is not a signed agreement and must not be used as legal advice.
Owner: Founder/CEO with counsel.
Scope: Employees, contractors, advisors, interns, and vendors only after role-specific review.
Review cadence: Before every use, and whenever hiring state, contractor location, role type, or equity/IP structure changes.

## Purpose

Blueprint's durable value depends on real-site capture supply, rights-safe provenance, package workflows, hosted access, buyer relationships, agent instructions, and source-of-truth discipline. This packet defines the business terms counsel should turn into enforceable documents.

## Intended Agreement Set

Counsel/PEO should decide whether to use one agreement or separate documents:

- confidentiality agreement;
- proprietary information and inventions assignment agreement;
- offer letter or contractor services agreement;
- data protection addendum where the person handles sensitive customer/capture/employee data;
- equipment and access acknowledgment;
- handbook acknowledgment.

## Confidential Information

Confidential information includes non-public information about:

- capture methods, raw bundles, manifests, timestamps, poses, device metadata, and provenance;
- rights/privacy/consent and commercialization records;
- site-specific world-model packages, hosted-session artifacts, and buyer-facing proof packages;
- buyer, site-operator, capturer, partner, vendor, and employee data;
- pricing, revenue, GTM, outbound, and pipeline information;
- source code, deployment details, credentials, security controls, and architecture;
- Paperclip issues, agent runs, Notion mirrors, internal strategy, and operating metrics;
- product plans, model-provider evaluations, and roadmap decisions.

## Exclusions

Counsel should define standard exclusions for information that is publicly available through no breach, already known without restriction, independently developed without using Blueprint confidential information, or lawfully received from a third party.

## Obligations

The recipient should agree to:

- use confidential information only for authorized Blueprint work;
- protect it with at least reasonable care and the security controls Blueprint requires;
- avoid disclosure without written approval;
- keep secrets out of unmanaged tools, public repos, screenshots, and AI prompts not approved for the data class;
- report suspected disclosure or compromise immediately;
- return or securely delete information at offboarding or request, subject to legal hold instructions.

## IP And Work Product

Counsel should draft assignment language covering:

- inventions, source code, docs, designs, workflows, prompts, agent kits, data schemas, models, training/eval assets, GTM systems, and operating processes created for Blueprint;
- improvements to capture, pipeline, WebApp, hosted-session, rights/privacy/provenance, Paperclip, and Notion workflows;
- moral rights waivers where allowed;
- prior invention disclosure where required by state law;
- contractor work-made-for-hire and assignment fallback language.

## Third-Party And Open-Source Materials

No person may add third-party code, data, designs, media, model outputs, or licensed material to Blueprint deliverables unless the license and source are compatible with Blueprint use. Open-source or external materials must be recorded in the relevant repo or Paperclip issue.

## AI-Generated Outputs

AI outputs are work artifacts, not proof by themselves. Generated text, images, code, research, or summaries may be used only when:

- source inputs are allowed for that tool;
- no confidential or regulated data is sent to an unapproved tool;
- claims remain backed by repo/runtime/capture evidence;
- generated outputs are reviewed before external use.

## Return And Deletion

At offboarding, the person must return company equipment and remove company data from personal devices, unmanaged drives, notes, and accounts unless counsel issues different instructions.

## Agreement Metadata

Counsel should include:

- party names and role type;
- governing law and forum;
- employment-at-will preservation where applicable;
- state-specific carveouts;
- whistleblower, protected activity, and lawful disclosure carveouts;
- effective date and signature process;
- employee copy delivery and retention process.

Counsel/PEO must review state-specific employment law, contractor classification, IP assignment enforceability, confidentiality carveouts, privacy obligations, and signature mechanics before this packet is issued.
