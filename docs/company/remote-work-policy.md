# Remote Work Policy

Status: Draft, requires counsel/PEO review before employee rollout.
Owner: Founder/CEO with managers and Security Procurement Agent.
Scope: Remote employees, contractors, interns, and managers.
Review cadence: Quarterly, and before hiring in a new location.

## Purpose

Blueprint is remote-first for most work and field-capable when capture, buyer, site, or vendor work requires it. Remote work must support fast execution, clear documentation, secure data handling, and source-of-truth discipline.

## Work Expectations

Remote team members must:

- maintain reliable internet and a secure workspace;
- protect company devices and data from unauthorized access;
- keep calendar and availability current;
- attend required meetings and manager check-ins;
- document decisions and evidence in the right system;
- avoid using personal tools for restricted company data;
- communicate blockers promptly.

## Availability

Managers define role-specific availability expectations. Default expectations:

- publish working hours and timezone;
- respond to urgent operational or security issues promptly during agreed hours;
- use asynchronous updates for non-urgent work;
- record handoffs when work depends on another person or agent.

## Location And Employment Compliance

Work location affects payroll, tax, workers' compensation, leave, benefits, employment notices, and new-hire reporting. A new hire may not start work from a new state or country until HR/PEO/counsel confirms the employment setup.

Employees must notify their manager before changing primary work location.

## Secure Workspace

Remote workspace requirements:

- screen lock when away;
- private handling of sensitive calls and documents;
- no unattended company devices in public spaces;
- no shared family or roommate access to company accounts;
- use approved storage for company files;
- report lost devices or suspected compromise immediately.

## Field And Travel Work

Remote-first does not mean field-free. Some roles may support:

- real-site capture;
- buyer or site-operator meetings;
- equipment shipment/setup;
- vendor or investor meetings;
- conferences or hiring events.

Field work must follow expense, security, rights/privacy/provenance, and manager approval rules.

## Performance

Remote performance is judged by outcomes, quality, evidence, and collaboration, not by performative availability. Managers should use role scorecards and 30/60/90 plans in `docs/onboarding/role-scorecards-and-30-60-90.md`.

## Tools

Use approved company systems for company work:

- GitHub/repo for code and canonical docs;
- Paperclip for execution state and closeouts;
- Notion for workspace/review/visibility;
- Slack/email/calendar for communication;
- approved payroll/benefits/vendor systems for employment administration.

## Evidence Paths

- Work output: repo, app/runtime, Paperclip issue, or approved artifact.
- Work visibility: Notion mirrors and Work Queue.
- Location/HR records: HR/PEO/counsel-controlled system.
- Policy: `docs/company/remote-work-policy.md`.
