# Blueprint Code Of Conduct

Status: Draft, requires counsel/PEO review before employee rollout.
Owner: Founder/CEO with Ops Lead.
Scope: Employees, founders, interns, contractors, advisors, and AI-agent operators when acting on Blueprint work.
Review cadence: Quarterly, and after any substantiated conduct incident.

## Purpose

Blueprint's work combines real-site capture evidence, robotics buyers, operational automation, and sensitive company/customer systems. A high-trust culture is not optional. This code defines expected behavior and the escalation path for conduct issues.

## Standards

Everyone working for Blueprint must:

- treat coworkers, candidates, customers, site contacts, vendors, and capturers with respect;
- be honest about product state, source evidence, rights/privacy status, and operational blockers;
- protect confidential, customer, employee, and capture information;
- use company systems only for legitimate company work;
- raise concerns early when a decision may affect legal, HR, finance, security, rights/privacy, or buyer commitments.

## Prohibited Conduct

The following conduct is prohibited:

- harassment, discrimination, intimidation, threats, or retaliation;
- unwelcome conduct based on protected traits under applicable law;
- abusive, demeaning, or humiliating communication in work channels;
- knowingly false reports, fabricated evidence, or invented operational proof;
- unauthorized access to systems, accounts, source repositories, customer data, capture data, or employee information;
- misuse of AI tools to impersonate people, bypass review gates, scrape private systems, or generate unsupported claims;
- retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith.

## Harassment And Discrimination

Blueprint intends to follow applicable anti-discrimination and anti-harassment law. Employees should report concerns to a manager, founder, Ops Lead, HR/PEO contact once configured, or counsel-directed intake path.

Managers who receive a report must:

- listen without arguing or minimizing;
- preserve the relevant facts, messages, timestamps, and participants;
- avoid retaliation;
- escalate to HR/PEO/counsel for protected-class, safety, retaliation, or severe conduct concerns;
- take reasonable interim steps to protect the reporting person and the integrity of the review.

## Remote And Digital Conduct

This code applies in Slack, email, GitHub, Notion, Paperclip, comments, calls, text messages, customer communications, generated docs, and any channel used for Blueprint work.

Remote work does not lower the standard. Written tone should be direct, respectful, and evidence-based. Sensitive people issues should move to the right private channel with HR/PEO/counsel support.

## AI-Agent Conduct

AI agents and agent operators must not:

- fabricate customers, recipients, captures, clearances, metrics, legal review, or deployment proof;
- use disallowed identities or mailboxes;
- make irreversible decisions without the named human gate;
- represent generated outputs as ground truth;
- treat Notion as execution truth when Paperclip or repo/runtime evidence owns the state.

Agent behavior issues should be captured in Paperclip with the run id, issue id, artifacts, and the specific policy violation.

## Reporting Path

Use the lowest-risk path that protects confidentiality and evidence:

1. Manager or founder for ordinary team concerns.
2. Ops Lead for operational or cross-functional concerns.
3. Security Procurement Agent for security, privacy, vendor, or data-handling concerns.
4. HR/PEO/counsel channel once configured for employment, protected-class, wage/hour, leave, benefits, accommodation, or retaliation concerns.
5. Paperclip blocker only when the concern is an operational blocker that can be described without exposing sensitive personal details.

## Investigation And Remediation

Blueprint will review reports promptly and take action appropriate to the issue. Actions may include coaching, access changes, reassignment, written warning, vendor termination, account suspension, legal review, or employment action.

Investigation notes involving people issues should be access-limited. Do not place sensitive personal details in public Notion pages or ordinary repo docs.

## Evidence Paths

- Policy: `docs/company/code-of-conduct.md`.
- Conduct-related operational blockers: Paperclip issue with restricted details where possible.
- Security/data conduct issues: `docs/company/incident-response-and-escalation.md`.
- Employee record: HR/PEO/counsel-controlled system once configured.

## Review

Review quarterly with counsel/PEO and after any report that reveals a policy gap.
