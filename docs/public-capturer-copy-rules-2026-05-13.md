# Public Capturer Copy Rules

Date: 2026-05-13

Status: Active public-copy guardrail

Scope: `/capture`, `/capture-app`, `/capture-app/launch-access`, `/signup/capturer`, and adjacent public capturer CTAs.

## Positioning

Lead with the plain opportunity:

> Get paid to capture real places robots need to understand.

The task should stay concrete:

- apply or request launch access
- receive an approved assignment where the payout is shown before starting
- walk a lawful public-facing route using the app
- upload one complete walkthrough
- wait for review

## Required Truth Boundaries

- Use conditional payout language: `accepted capture`, `assignment payout shown before you start`, `review required`, and `payout eligibility`.
- Do not claim guaranteed work, guaranteed approval, guaranteed payout, or guaranteed volume.
- Do not publish `$40 average` or any fixed average payout unless a repo-backed artifact or human approval explicitly supports it.
- Keep launch-city, invite, and access-code gates visible.
- Keep public-area-only, privacy-safe, and sensitive-zone avoidance language visible.
- Smart glasses are optional and aspirational: supported only for approved repeat walkthroughs where hardware, launch proof, and downstream capture proof exist.

## Research Calibration

Current external contributor and tasking surfaces generally make the contributor path simple, app-led, and gated:

- [Kled](https://www.kled.ai/) leads with opt-in paid data contribution and verified contributor data.
- [375ai](https://www.375.ai/) positions contribution around app/node participation, privacy-first collection, and rewards.
- [REscan](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/rescan-unveils-3d-mapping-technology-to-capture-and-digitize-physical-spaces-from-the-human-perspective-301052925.html) frames spatial capture as searchable environment data and digital twin infrastructure.
- [Acuity AI](https://acuityai.sh/) says a mobile app lets smartphone contributors submit video data at scale with quality control.
- [Uber Driver](https://www.uber.com/us/en/drive/), [DoorDash Dasher](https://dasher.doordash.com/en-us), [Taskrabbit](https://support.taskrabbit.com/hc/en-us/articles/46260467885979-How-Do-I-Become-a-Tasker), and [Instacart Shopper](https://www.instacart.com/help/section/809794019/2602051553) all make requirements, app workflow, city/availability constraints, and review or verification part of the worker path.
- [DoorDash Tasks](https://about.doordash.com/en-us/news/introducing-doordash-tasks) is the closest recent analogue: short real-world tasks, upfront pay based on effort/complexity, select-market availability, and data used for AI/robotics.

Blueprint should be sharper than those surfaces by naming the exact field action and stricter truth boundaries, not by promising broader earning volume.
