# Human New Hire Start Here

Status: Active onboarding draft, requires HR/PEO review for employment administration steps.
Owner: Hiring manager with Ops Lead.
Audience: New Blueprint employees, interns, and long-term contractors.
Review cadence: After each hire and quarterly.

## Welcome

Blueprint builds the buyer, licensing, ops, and hosted-access system for site-specific world-model products. The company is capture-first and world-model-product-first: real-site capture evidence, rights/privacy/provenance controls, site-specific packages, hosted access, and buyer workflows are the core.

Your first job is to understand where truth lives before changing anything.

## First-Day Goals

By the end of day one, you should know:

- what Blueprint sells and what it does not claim;
- the difference between capture truth, package artifacts, readiness outputs, and generated support material;
- which repo owns which part of the system;
- why Paperclip is execution truth;
- why Notion is the workspace/review/visibility surface;
- which systems you can access and which require approval;
- who your manager is and what your first-week outcome is.

## First-Week Goals

By the end of week one, you should have:

- completed required paperwork through HR/PEO/payroll path;
- completed access and equipment setup;
- reviewed the employee handbook and policy packet;
- met your manager and key collaborators;
- completed one small, evidence-backed task;
- recorded your work in the correct system;
- raised any source-needed gap or blocker instead of guessing.

This shape follows current onboarding practice guidance that emphasizes pre-start preparation, structured week-one agendas, manager ownership, buddy/reviewer support, and 30/60/90 outcomes. HR/legal administration still follows the policy packet and counsel/PEO-reviewed systems.

## What To Avoid

Do not:

- invent customer, capture, rights, readiness, revenue, or KPI proof;
- treat generated summaries as ground truth;
- move or delete Notion pages without proven replacement identity;
- run live-send, deploy, provider, payment, payroll, or production scripts without authorization;
- use unapproved tools for restricted data;
- promise benefits, PTO, payroll, equity, legal, or compliance terms outside approved documents.

## Systems

### Repo

Use repo files for doctrine, code, contracts, policy drafts, and implementation evidence.

### Paperclip

Use Paperclip for execution ownership, blockers, delegated runs, closeout evidence, and routine state.

### Notion

Use Notion for onboarding workspace, review, dashboards, and human-readable visibility. Notion should link back to repo/Paperclip evidence.

### Slack/Email

Use Slack/email for communication, not as the durable source of truth for execution or policy.

## First Task Pattern

For your first assigned task:

1. Confirm owner, goal, and source-of-truth system.
2. Read the relevant repo docs and current artifacts.
3. Check for dirty work or existing open work if you are editing a repo.
4. Make the smallest useful change.
5. Verify with the documented command.
6. Record evidence path and remaining gaps.

## People To Meet

Manager should schedule:

- manager 1:1;
- founder/CEO context session when practical;
- product doctrine walkthrough;
- security/access walkthrough;
- Paperclip/Notion source-of-truth walkthrough;
- role-specific buddy or reviewer.
