# Role Scorecards And 30/60/90 Plans

Status: Active onboarding draft.
Owner: Hiring manager with Founder/CEO and `blueprint-cto` for technical roles.
Scope: Human and AI-agent roles.
Review cadence: Before hiring, at 30/60/90 days, and quarterly.

## Purpose

Scorecards make onboarding outcome-based. A new hire should know what good work looks like, how it will be measured, where evidence lives, and which decisions require escalation.

## Common Scorecard Fields

Each role should define:

- mission;
- manager;
- source-of-truth systems;
- first-week task;
- 30-day outcomes;
- 60-day outcomes;
- 90-day outcomes;
- operating metrics and current source status;
- human gates;
- systems required;
- review cadence.

## Company-Wide Competencies

All roles are evaluated on:

- truthful product language;
- source-of-truth discipline;
- security and privacy judgment;
- clear blocker escalation;
- quality of evidence;
- collaboration and respectful conduct;
- willingness to mark source-needed gaps instead of guessing.

## Engineering Scorecard

Mission: improve WebApp, capture/pipeline contracts, hosted sessions, buyer workflows, and autonomous-org runtime without weakening provenance or live-state truth.

30 days:

- reads required doctrine without prompting;
- runs safe local checks;
- ships scoped changes with verification;
- preserves unrelated dirty work;
- explains high-risk files and testing requirements.

60 days:

- owns a bounded feature/fix;
- writes or updates focused tests;
- documents source-of-truth boundaries;
- handles Paperclip/Notion mirrors conservatively.

90 days:

- leads a safe refactor slice or product workflow improvement;
- diagnoses live/runtime blockers with evidence;
- mentors new agent/human contributors.

Metrics:

- shipped changes with verification: repo/GitHub/Paperclip;
- defect escape rate: source-needed until issue taxonomy is wired;
- review turnaround: source-needed until review telemetry is wired.

## Ops/Growth Scorecard

Mission: move capture supply, buyer demand, hosted-review starts, rights/provenance readiness, and customer operations with evidence-backed execution.

30 days:

- understands Exact-Site Hosted Review wedge;
- uses Work Queue and Paperclip correctly;
- distinguishes planned profiles from live proof;
- can prepare a truthful buyer or city-launch artifact.

60 days:

- owns a repeatable ops/growth lane;
- records recipient-backed contacts and avoids inferred emails;
- escalates rights/privacy/legal gaps correctly.

90 days:

- improves conversion, demand, or ops reliability with measured evidence;
- publishes review-ready Notion mirrors without making Notion execution truth.

Metrics:

- targets/replies/calls/hosted-review starts: source-needed until live sources are wired;
- proof packages shipped: source-needed until pipeline/WebApp artifact source is wired;
- blocker aging: Paperclip/Work Queue.

## Manager Scorecard

Mission: create role clarity, protect policy boundaries, and turn onboarding into useful output.

30 days:

- completes pre-start and first-week checklist;
- delivers feedback weekly;
- verifies access and policy acknowledgment;
- records source-needed gaps.

60 days:

- delegates a bounded workstream;
- resolves or escalates blockers;
- keeps role scorecard current.

90 days:

- completes review;
- adjusts scope or support plan;
- documents whether role is ready for normal operating cadence.

## AI-Agent Scorecard

Mission: execute assigned work with source-of-truth safety, bounded side effects, and evidence-grade closeouts.

30 days:

- reads required docs;
- starts with `git status --short` for repo work;
- uses safe commands;
- records evidence and gaps.

60 days:

- handles a lane with limited supervision;
- respects Notion/Paperclip boundaries;
- avoids unsupported live claims.

90 days:

- can run a recurring or issue-bound lane with clear closeout evidence;
- escalates human gates accurately;
- leaves repo and Notion surfaces cleaner than found.

## Review Prompts

Use these at each milestone:

- What did the person/agent ship?
- What evidence proves it?
- Which source-needed gaps remain?
- Which access or policy issues slowed them down?
- Which source-of-truth mistake is most likely next?
- What should change before the next review?
