## Lawful Capture Supply Acquisition System

Blueprint’s supply acquisition in Durham relies on vetted, professional AEC cohorts. The system must follow this procedural guide:

1. **Profile Identification:** The `capturer-growth-agent` targets licensed land surveyors and GIS (Geographic Information System) mapping specialists (e.g., Summit Design and Engineering Services personnel). 
2. **Access Path Authorization & Pre-Dispatch ITAR Screening:** The Blueprint operator must secure a signed `site_operator_intro` consent form granting formal permission from the facility management. Crucially, the site operator must explicitly verify in writing that the target bay contains no ITAR-restricted, export-controlled, or defense-adjacent materials *before* any capturer is assigned. 
3. **Credential Verification (Trust Packet):** Target capturers submit professional documentation, including Certificate of Liability Insurance and hardware specifications, explicitly agreeing to Blueprint's data-resale terms (pending verification of NC Board of Examiners IP rules).
4. **Dispatch and Capture Boundaries:** A hard-capped starter cohort (1 to 3 AEC professionals) is authorized to capture. They are given a strict boundary limit (e.g., column line A to column line F, not to exceed 10,000 sq ft) to upload via `BlueprintCapture`. 
5. **Physical Boundary Enforcement (Critical Operational Constraint):** To prevent inadvertent capture of restricted defense/export-controlled items sitting just beyond column line F inside an open-floor 3PL warehouse, the operation demands strict physical enforcement during the capture event. This must be executed by providing an on-site Blueprint chaperone, erecting temporary physical sightline barriers around the 10,000 sq ft perimeter, or engaging explicit hardware-level range-limiting on the surveyor's laser scanner prior to operation.

## Rights / Provenance / Privacy Clearance System

Because Durham’s industrial facilities may overlap with RTP defense contractors, rights and provenance are treated as absolute stop conditions at the intake phase.

The clearance system acts as a strict human gate:
1. **Operator Review (Pre-Capture):** The designated human rights reviewer manually examines the `site_operator_intro` authorization. If the operator refuses to certify the absence of export-controlled zones, the site triggers an immediate `blocked` status.
2. **Trust Packet Submission:** Capturers upload proof of identity and sign IP assignments granting Blueprint distribution rights.
3. **Rights Stamps:** Post-capture, the `rights-provenance-agent` processes the initial metadata, verifying temporal alignment, device signatures, and geofencing limits to ensure the capturer did not exceed the 10,000 sq ft boundary.
4. **Pipeline Stop Conditions:** If raw capture metadata exceeds the authorized spatial bounds or lacks explicit consent flags for commercialization, `BlueprintCapturePipeline` is policy-blocked from materializing the package.
