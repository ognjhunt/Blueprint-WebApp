## Launch Thesis
Chicago is a large, economically diverse market with deep industrial, logistics, and construction sectors that could benefit from Blueprint's capture-first world model products. However, compared to Austin, Chicago's trust graph is less dense for early cohort seeding, its robotics community is more institutional than founder-driven, and its commercial real estate and construction markets operate through entrenched brokerage and contractor networks that may not be as receptive to a new spatial data startup without significant relationship-based introduction.

Chicago should serve as a strategic second-city candidate only after Austin validates the gated-cohort model, or in response to unsolicited demand from a qualified Chicago anchor contact.

## Why Chicago (Not Yet as First City)
- evidence-backed: the city-launch proof framework (capturer signup with access-source routing, structured analytics events) exists but has not been validated end-to-end in any city yet
- inferred: Chicago's larger market size and more complex geography make it harder to test cohort dynamics cleanly than a smaller, relationship-dense city like Austin
- inferred: Chicago's industrial, logistics, and construction buyers operate through mature procurement channels that expect proof-of-value before engagement
- inferred: robotics and AI talent in Chicago is institutional (universities, corporate labs) rather than community-driven, making organic seeding harder
- evidence-backed: supply-intel playbook references a Chicago logistics flyer distribution partner (Oppizi) but this is a single tactical channel, not a full capturer acquisition strategy

## Recommended Launch Posture
- do not begin active Chicago capturer recruiting until Austin gated-cohort pilot proves activation and first-capture quality signals
- treat Chicago as a response-ready market: if a qualified Chicago-side contact emerges, handle it through the intake-agent with full access-source tagging
- keep all Chicago-related planning as background research, not active outreach
- use Chicago's industrial and logistics density as a strategic reference point for eventual expansion, not as a launch driver

## Target Capturer Profile (When Expansion-Ready)
- industrial facility operators or logistics coordinators with recurring site-change needs
- construction project managers working on multi-phase commercial or mixed-use developments
- commercial real estate teams doing building documentation or tenant improvement planning
- reachable through an institutional partnership, industry association, or trusted business introduction
- willing to accept gated access and human approval before any capture work begins

## Sequencing Recommendation
Chicago should follow Austin in the launch queue. The correct sequence is:

1. Validate gated-cohort model in Austin (capturer signup, activation, first-capture quality, trust infrastructure)
2. Build city-specific analytics and instrumentation based on Austin lessons
3. Only then evaluate Chicago as a multi-channel expansion target with institutional partnership strategy

Chicago remains "planning only" until steps 1 and 2 are complete. The current total readiness score of 14/30 confirms this posture.
