# San Francisco, CA — Blueprint City Launch Plan

## Status
- phase: planning
- owner: city-launch-agent
- last-human-launch-decision: not approved

## Why San Francisco
- extremely dense robotics, AI, startup, and technical talent market
- high strategic signal value if Blueprint can activate trusted capturers here
- likely strong overlap with both buyers and prospective capturers

## Initial Hypotheses
- SF may offer the richest high-intent technical supply pool
- competition for attention is higher, so generic recruiting copy will underperform
- trust, positioning, and relevance may matter more than broad-volume posting

## Likely Channel Mix
- robotics / AI operator communities
- creator and technical freelancer communities
- startup / builder community channels
- high-trust referral loops from approved contributors

## Risks
- noisy market with many competing opportunities
- higher expectations from technically sophisticated contributors
- generic gig-style messaging could damage trust quickly

## Missing Data
- response quality from SF-specific technical channels
- realistic ops burden if supply comes from highly selective contributors
- whether SF should be prioritized for density or for signal value

## Dependencies
- city-specific landing / recruiting copy
- instrumentation for city-level cohort quality
- clear human-reviewed policy on any incentive language

## Next Actions
1. refine SF-specific channel ranking
2. define which value prop should lead in SF
3. create the first SF readiness scorecard
