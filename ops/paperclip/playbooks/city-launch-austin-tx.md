# Austin, TX — Blueprint City Launch Plan

## Status
- phase: planning
- owner: city-launch-agent
- last-human-launch-decision: not approved

## Why Austin
- strong technical, robotics, creator, and startup density
- founder/operator reach is likely stronger than in a random city
- should be easier to test community-led channels before paid acquisition

## Initial Hypotheses
- local robotics and builder communities may produce higher-trust early capturers
- Austin may be a better first "relationship-driven" city than a pure volume city
- channel quality may beat raw volume if the messaging stays honest and technical

## Likely Channel Mix
- robotics and maker communities
- university technical communities
- local creator / production freelancer groups
- founder-network introductions into trusted local communities

## Risks
- signup volume may be lower than a broader gig-market approach
- founder-network sourcing may not scale
- quality may be good but density could still be slow without referral loops

## Missing Data
- real Austin channel response rates
- local supply quality by source
- city-specific ops burden once the first cohort activates

## Dependencies
- city-specific landing / recruiting copy
- attribution and funnel instrumentation by city
- referral loop design
- human review of any city-specific incentive offer

## Next Actions
1. validate Austin-specific channels against supply-intel findings
2. define Austin messaging variations for review
3. create a launch scorecard with activation and quality thresholds
