# San Francisco, CA — Blueprint City Demand Plan

## Status
- phase: planning
- owner: city-demand-agent
- last-human-demand-decision: not approved

## Why San Francisco
- dense concentration of robotics, autonomy, simulation, and world-model-adjacent teams
- strong strategic value for exact-site product positioning and fast technical feedback
- likely overlap between startup R&D, deployment planning, and infrastructure tooling buyers

## City Demand Thesis
San Francisco is likely the highest-density market for technically sophisticated robot-team buyers, but demand quality will depend on whether Blueprint can present exact-site proof and hosted access without sounding like another generic AI infrastructure pitch.

## Likely Buyer Clusters
- robotics startups and autonomy teams needing real-site evaluation assets
- simulation and data teams that need site-grounded artifacts
- deployment teams preparing for customer-site rollouts
- systems integrators and technical operators seeking exact-site proof before field commitments

## Relevant Facility Hypotheses
- robotics demo, test, and deployment environments
- logistics, commercial, and mixed-use facilities where layout specificity matters
- customer sites that require tighter governance or operator coordination

These are working hypotheses, not confirmed demand.

## Channel and Community Hypotheses
- Bay Area robotics and autonomy communities
- technical events where exact-site proof can be discussed credibly
- founder and operator introductions into deployment-heavy startups
- system-integrator and technical partner networks

## Optional Site-Operator Opportunity Lane
- more likely to surface in sensitive or high-value facilities where access and commercialization are tightly controlled
- should remain a secondary branch unless a specific buyer motion clearly depends on it

## Proof-Pack Expectations
- buyers are likely to demand sharper distinctions between what is real now and what is aspirational
- hosted-session proof should be technically legible and exact-site specific
- provenance, privacy, and commercialization boundaries should be explicit early

## Provisional Readiness Scorecard
- likely robot-team density: 5/5
- exact-site proof fit: 4/5
- access and commercialization opportunity: 3/5
- instrumentation readiness: 2/5
- operational follow-through readiness: 2/5
- strategic importance: 5/5

## Risks
- high buyer density can still produce shallow demand if the product story sounds generic
- sophisticated buyers may expose packaging and hosted-session gaps faster than Austin buyers
- operator-side complexity could appear earlier in high-value site conversations

## Missing Data
- which Bay Area buyer clusters care most about exact-site assets today
- whether hosted-session proof is enough to move serious follow-up without deeper commercial scaffolding
- where operator-lane needs show up first across likely facility types

## Dependencies
- city-specific buyer copy and proof-pack framing
- strong instrumentation for proof-review and hosted-session follow-up
- a clean human handoff for pricing, contracts, procurement, and commercialization questions
- clearer operator-lane escalation paths for sensitive facility cases

## Next Actions
1. rank San Francisco buyer clusters by exact-site need and technical proof expectations
2. define which proof assets should lead in Bay Area buyer conversations
3. create the first SF-specific dependency list for web, analytics, ops, and human commercial handoff
