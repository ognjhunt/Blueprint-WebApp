## Why San Francisco
- evidence-backed: BLU-150 found the Bay Area has stronger buyer-matchmaking and commercialization surfaces than Austin through BARA-style closed-door startup, corporate, investor, and supplier interactions.
- evidence-backed: Bay Area robotics density still makes San Francisco the clearest city for technical feedback on proof-pack and hosted-review quality.
- inferred: San Francisco remains strategically important because it can expose both buyer appetite and product weaknesses faster than a thinner market.

## City Demand Thesis
San Francisco remains the highest-density near-term city for technically sophisticated robot-team demand, but the useful motion is still narrow: proof-led commercialization conversations through robotics communities and buyer-matchmaking channels, not broad AI awareness or generic startup hype.

## What Changed This Pass
- evidence-backed: BARA-style buyer and supplier matchmaking is now a concrete SF-specific channel signal instead of a vague community hypothesis
- evidence-backed: BLU-150 reinforces that qualified demand clusters around rapid proof review, remote inspection-style hosted review, and stack-adjacent partner channels
- evidence-backed: the city-launch work in this repo now uses explicit access-source routing and remains gated, so San Francisco demand planning should stay narrow and exact-site grounded rather than drifting into generic public recruiting language
- inherited: San Francisco now explicitly inherits the reusable proof-pack structure, hosted-review standard, artifact handoff checklist, and buyer follow-up expectation from `robot-team-demand-playbook.md`
- inferred: the generic robot-team proof-path and funnel work are already represented in [BLU-170](/BLU/issues/BLU-170) and [BLU-190](/BLU/issues/BLU-190), so this city pass should focus on city-specific routing and escalation gaps instead of duplicating the base proof system
- inferred: San Francisco can support a denser commercialization motion than Austin, but only if Blueprint stays exact-site and operationally legible

## Likely Buyer Clusters
- primary: robotics startups and autonomy teams needing real-site evaluation assets before field commitments
- primary: deployment and commercialization teams preparing for customer-site rollouts and partner conversations
- secondary: simulation and data teams that need site-grounded artifacts tied to real operations
- secondary: systems integrators, industrial-data partners, and technical operators evaluating exact-site proof before deeper rollout work

## Relevant Facility Hypotheses
- robotics demo, test, and deployment environments where buyers need remote proof before on-site work
- logistics, commercial, and mixed-use facilities where layout specificity matters to rollout risk
- customer sites with tighter governance or operator coordination requirements, especially when commercialization is sensitive

These remain working hypotheses, not confirmed demand.

## Evidence-Backed Channel Anchors
- Bay Area Robotics Association-style closed-door matchmaking and buyer/supplier introductions
- proof-led technical events similar to Foxglove Actuate where data, debugging, and deployment workflows are central
- founder and operator introductions into deployment-heavy startups
- systems-integrator and industrial-data partner networks that shorten rollout and data-integration friction

## City-Specific Channel Inferences
- San Francisco can likely support more community density and commercialization surface area than Austin
- generic AI or world-model positioning will likely underperform quickly with this audience unless tied to exact-site proof and current-stack compatibility

## Optional Site-Operator Opportunity Lane
- operator-secondary: partner-led, systems-integrator-led, and exact-site buyer motions remain the default
- operator-relevant but still secondary: sensitive or high-value facilities where commercialization and access governance are tighter and surface earlier
- human review still owns any commercialization language, pricing, rights, or permission question

## Proof-Pack Expectations
- San Francisco inherits the shared proof-pack order from `robot-team-demand-playbook.md`: buyer context, site evidence, review-now, artifact handoff, and gap/escalation
- buyers will demand sharper distinctions between what is real now and what is aspirational
- hosted review needs to be technically legible, exact-site specific, and easy to inspect remotely, with artifact handoff ready for async review
- Blueprint should show a fast proof path, ideally same day or within 24 hours, rather than a long bespoke setup
- provenance, recency, privacy, rights, and commercialization boundaries should be explicit early
- Blueprint should label what exists now versus what would require extra capture, packaging, integration, or approvals
- the default follow-up after a serious review should be the shared artifact-led recap within one business day, not a generic sales note

## San Francisco-Specific Proof Deltas
- partner- and matchmaking-led motions make the artifact-handoff block more important here; buyers need to understand what can be reviewed asynchronously inside their current stack before they agree to deeper follow-up
- the buyer-context block should call out whether the motion came from BARA-style matchmaking, a proof-led event, a founder intro, or a partner referral because those sources imply different follow-up expectations
- commercialization-adjacent buyers in SF will scrutinize the gap/escalation block quickly, so operator, privacy, rights, and human-gated questions should be surfaced earlier instead of deferred
- SF can support a denser proof volume than Austin, but only if hosted review stays standardized enough that sophisticated buyers see a technical evaluation surface rather than a bespoke sales demo

## San Francisco-Specific Proof Gaps
- no San Francisco-ready proof-pack template currently forces stack-compatibility notes, async artifact review expectations, and explicit human-gated commercialization boundaries into the same packet
- no ops-owned SF checklist yet exists for turning buyer-matchmaking or partner intros into same-day hosted review plus artifact-led follow-up
- no SF-specific measurement currently tracks the inherited proof motion by source across `proof_pack_delivered`, `hosted_review_started`, and `hosted_review_follow_up_sent`
- no explicit growth guidance yet distinguishes which SF buyer clusters can move on adjacent-site proof versus which ones require exact-site proof immediately
