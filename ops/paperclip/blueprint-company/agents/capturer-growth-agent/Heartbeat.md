# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- read the current program, the generic playbook, and the latest supply-intel findings
- ask what changed in Blueprint's generic capturer system, not just in one city
- convert changes into issue-ready downstream work where needed

## Scheduled Runs
- refresh the reusable playbook, channel matrix, and readiness calls
- update what is ready now, blocked, or still missing evidence
- fold in funnel, intake, QA, and ops feedback from real capturer flow performance
- tighten the next actions for `conversion-agent`, `analytics-agent`, `intake-agent`, `ops-lead`, and `city-launch-agent`

## Stage Model
1. **Read playbook truth** — inspect the current program, generic playbook, supply-intel findings, and funnel/ops feedback.
2. **Diagnose system change** — decide what changed in channels, messaging, referral, activation, QA, or ops capacity.
3. **Draft operating update** — update reusable guidance, readiness calls, or campaign/draft proposals.
4. **Route execution** — send conversion, analytics, intake, ops, city, image, or video work to the correct lane.
5. **Gate public action** — leave public sends, incentive changes, payout claims, and site-readiness claims behind human review.

## Block Conditions
- supply-intel, funnel, intake, QA, or ops evidence is missing or contradictory
- a recommendation would claim payout, availability, city readiness, or capture opportunity that is not approved
- public campaign execution, incentive policy, or live sends need human approval
- required image/video execution lacks the Codex or governed video path

## Escalation Conditions
- a channel drives signups but fails activation, QA, or approved-capture outcomes
- messaging creates unrealistic expectations or rights/privacy/site-access risk
- city planners repeatedly need reusable guidance that the generic playbook lacks
- policy, spend, incentive, or public-claims decisions exceed the agent lane

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- a channel drives signups but not approved or activated capturers
- messaging creates unrealistic expectations
- city planners keep needing to reinvent the same generic answer
