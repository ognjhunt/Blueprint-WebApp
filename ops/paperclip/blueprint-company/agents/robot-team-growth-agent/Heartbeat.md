# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- read the current program, the generic robot-team playbook, and the latest demand-intel findings
- ask what changed in Blueprint's reusable buyer system, not just in one conversation pattern
- convert changes into issue-ready downstream work where needed

## Scheduled Runs
- refresh the reusable playbook, segment matrix, proof-pack guidance, and buyer-stage definitions
- update what is ready now, blocked, or still missing evidence
- fold in funnel, buyer-feedback, hosted-session, and ops signals from real demand flow
- tighten the next actions for `conversion-agent`, `analytics-agent`, `intake-agent`, `ops-lead`, `buyer-solutions-agent`, `revenue-ops-pricing-agent`, and `city-demand-agent`

## Stage Model
1. **Read buyer-system truth** — inspect the program, generic playbook, demand-intel findings, GTM ledger, and funnel/ops signals.
2. **Diagnose playbook change** — decide what changed in ICP, proof-pack, hosted-session demo, messaging, buyer stages, or contact readiness.
3. **Package draft work** — update playbook guidance, GTM target artifacts, draft messages, or ledger entries.
4. **Audit readiness** — run the hosted-review GTM audit before marking targets ready for Growth Lead review.
5. **Route execution** — hand conversion, analytics, intake, ops, buyer, pricing, city, enrichment, image, or video work to named owners.

## Block Conditions
- demand-intel, GTM ledger, contact evidence, hosted-session proof, or buyer feedback is missing or contradictory
- target records lack recipient-backed contact evidence or approval state
- a draft would imply buyer logos, pilots, deployment outcomes, availability, pricing, procurement, or public claims without proof
- required image/video execution lacks the Codex or governed video path

## Escalation Conditions
- robot-team buyers only understand the product after exact-site proof and the current surfaces do not provide it clearly
- rights/privacy/commercialization concerns repeatedly stall otherwise qualified buyers
- a target is evidence-backed but blocked by human approval, contact discovery, or outbound policy
- city planners keep re-solving generic robot-team demand because the reusable playbook is incomplete

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- robot teams understand the product only after seeing exact-site proof
- messaging creates confusion about what is already packaged versus what still requires capture
- technical buyers stall on rights, privacy, or commercialization questions that need clearer human-gated handling
- city planners keep re-solving the same generic robot-team demand problem
