# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- ground on the current Paperclip issue and the weekly reporting window
- verify that each claimed update maps back to a real shipped change, closed issue, or measured signal
- create draft artifacts and internal review breadcrumbs
- finish only after the [$humanizer](/Users/nijelhunt_1/.agents/skills/humanizer/SKILL.md) pass

## Scheduled Runs
- highlight the handful of changes the community will actually care about
- include one small metrics section if the numbers are real and interpretable
- end with one clear ask, preview, or invitation instead of a generic sign-off

## Stage Model
1. **Bind week** — confirm the issue, week, source program, and draft destination.
2. **Select proof** — choose only shipped changes, closed issues, measured signals, and real community/customer evidence.
3. **Draft package** — create draft artifacts, asset metadata, allowed/blocked claims, and internal review breadcrumbs.
4. **Route assets** — send image work to `webapp-codex` and scoped video work through the governed video path.
5. **Humanize and review** — run the anti-AI pass and leave the draft waiting for the right review gate.

## Block Conditions
- the strongest claims are unsupported by shipped work, closed issues, measured signals, or customer/community evidence
- the update would imply live availability, traction, launch status, or capability that Blueprint cannot prove
- draft writers, asset metadata, or review paths are unavailable
- final visuals or video are needed but no governed execution issue exists

## Escalation Conditions
- a draft needs live send, public publish, brand posture, or external-claim approval
- community-facing claims conflict with rights, provenance, hosted-session, or product truth
- repeated weekly updates lack user-visible substance and need growth/product reprioritization
- the best story is a risk or miss that needs founder/growth-lead positioning judgment

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- the week had lots of internal churn but little user-visible change
- the best evidence lives in ops or Firehose rather than product commits
- a draft starts sounding like a launch thread when the work was incremental
- the strongest story is actually a risk, miss, or lesson
