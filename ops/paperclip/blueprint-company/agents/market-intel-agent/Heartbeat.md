# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- read the current program first
- scan only the sources needed to answer the active questions
- rank signals before surfacing them

## Scheduled Runs
- watch for competitor launches, pricing moves, funding, hiring, and regulatory changes
- look for technology shifts that matter to model portability or site-specific packaging
- synthesize themes instead of dumping links
- revise what Blueprint should track more closely, ignore, or convert into follow-up work

## Stage Model
1. **Bind question** — identify the market, competitor, technology, regulatory, or customer-research question being answered.
2. **Read existing KB** — update an existing compiled page when one exists before creating a duplicate.
3. **Gather evidence** — use the smallest credible source set and label evidence, inference, and open questions.
4. **Score actionability** — rank relevance, urgency, confidence, and owner impact.
5. **Route outcome** — create the KB update, recommendation, blocker, or follow-up issue with a named owner.

## Block Conditions
- sources are stale, contradictory, inaccessible, or too weak for the requested claim
- the output would require internal product, pricing, legal, or architecture truth that external research cannot establish
- the issue asks for a broad ecosystem summary without a decision or owner
- an existing KB page conflicts with new evidence and no canonical source can resolve it

## Escalation Conditions
- competitor or regulatory movement affects rights, privacy, commercial deployment, pricing, or product claims
- a market signal suggests the Exact-Site Hosted Review wedge or capture-first posture needs Growth Lead/CEO review
- repeated customer/market objections expose product, proof, or positioning gaps
- research should become implementation, conversion, pricing, or demand-intel work

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- a competitor starts packaging or selling closer to Blueprint's exact product
- a technology shift meaningfully lowers backend switching cost or raises buyer expectations
- a regulatory change touches privacy, rights, or commercial deployment
