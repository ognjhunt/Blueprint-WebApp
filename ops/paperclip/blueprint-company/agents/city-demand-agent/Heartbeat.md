# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- read the city-demand program, the generic robot-team playbook, the operator-lane playbook, the active city docs, and the newest research
- check whether Austin or San Francisco changed materially since the last pass
- update scorecards and downstream issues before writing summary language

## Scheduled Runs
- refresh both city demand plans end to end
- rank the next dependencies by what unlocks the clearest robot-team buyer motion
- fold in new demand-intel, robot-team-growth, site-operator, analytics, intake, and ops feedback
- tighten or loosen city hypotheses only when the evidence moved

## Stage Model
1. **Bind city scope** — confirm whether Austin, San Francisco, or a specific GTM target context is in scope.
2. **Read demand evidence** — inspect robot-team playbook, operator-lane playbook, active city docs, newest research, and GTM ledger context.
3. **Update scorecard** — change city readiness only when buyer, facility, proof, ops, or instrumentation evidence moved.
4. **Route dependencies** — create downstream asks for robot-team growth, site-operator, analytics, intake, ops, or human gates.
5. **Close planning loop** — update the city demand plan and issue with exact blockers, next owner, and deferred claims.

## Block Conditions
- city buyer evidence, active city docs, GTM ledger, or newest research is missing or contradictory
- a plan would imply live city support, guaranteed demand, public posting, outreach, pricing, or availability without proof/approval
- operator-lane, rights/privacy, access, or commercialization questions require human review
- instrumentation or hosted-review proof is missing and no downstream owner is assigned

## Escalation Conditions
- one city shows materially clearer robot-team buyer concentration or proof requirements
- city demand is blocked by missing analytics, intake, ops, proof assets, hosted reviews, or human approval
- site-operator opportunity appears in a specific city and needs optional-lane review
- Exact-Site Hosted Review targets cannot advance because city/site context or recipient-backed contacts are missing

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- one city shows a clearer concentration of likely robot-team buyers
- hosted-session proof requirements differ materially by city or facility type
- operator-lane opportunity appears in one city without changing the default capture posture
- instrumentation or human approval gaps become the actual blocker
