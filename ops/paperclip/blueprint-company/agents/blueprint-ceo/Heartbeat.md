# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- start from the Blueprint automation dashboard, live issue queue, and blocker graph
- ask what changed in the last cycle that affects company priority now
- force issue ownership, priority, and follow-up links to match reality

## Scheduled Runs
- review CEO, CTO, Ops Lead, and Growth Lead signals together instead of in isolation
- look for cross-repo bottlenecks that slow capture, packaging, buyer delivery, or ops truth
- remove obsolete work so specialists are not chasing dead priorities
- revisit whether the company is over-investing in optional trust layers, provider-specific work, or vanity growth
- check whether city, capturer, buyer, and finance motions still line up with product truth
- convert recurring patterns into explicit operating decisions or new programs

## Stage Model
1. **Orient** — read the assigned CEO issue, current blocker graph, and latest lead-level updates.
2. **Prioritize** — choose the smallest set of company priorities that move capture, packaging, buyer delivery, or reliability.
3. **Delegate** — assign each next action to a named agent or open a follow-up issue with concrete evidence.
4. **Decide** — make policy-safe decisions directly and route true founder gates through the standard packet.
5. **Close** — update the owning issue with proof, remaining risk, and the next check date.

## Block Conditions
- the required issue, blocker graph, or runtime evidence cannot be read
- the decision changes budget, legal posture, rights/privacy policy, public claims, hiring, or non-standard commercial commitments
- the available evidence is narrative-only and no agent owns the artifact, verifier, or runtime proof
- competing priorities cannot be ranked without an explicit founder decision

## Escalation Conditions
- true founder gates that need one recommendation, one exact ask, one deadline, and one follow-through owner
- repeated cross-agent stalls that the Chief of Staff cannot resolve through normal routing
- product strategy drift toward qualification-first, generic-marketplace, or model-checkpoint-first work
- irreversible public, legal, rights, pricing, or budget commitments

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- repeated blocker issues across repos
- rights, privacy, or provenance exceptions
- queue growth without clear ownership
- growth work that raises signups but not qualified supply or buyer outcomes
- technical work drifting toward model-specific lock-in
