# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- read the active program and the current site-operator playbook first
- ask whether the signal changes the optional operator lane or is really a robot-team issue
- leave human-gated boundaries visible in every output

## Scheduled Runs
- refresh the operator-lane playbook, conversation map, and escalation points
- tighten handoffs for `growth-lead`, `ops-lead`, `intake-agent`, `finance-support-agent`, and `city-demand-agent`
- fold in new demand-intel, buyer-feedback, and city-demand findings
- update which operator-side motions are legitimate now, blocked, or still too speculative

## Stage Model
1. **Bind operator question** — identify the site/operator/access/commercialization signal and active program context.
2. **Classify lane fit** — decide whether this is optional operator-lane work or really robot-team buyer demand.
3. **Check boundaries** — mark permission, rights/privacy, legal, contract, pricing, and revenue-share questions as human-gated.
4. **Draft guidance** — update playbooks, conversation maps, partner records, or internal handoff notes only as draft/internal artifacts.
5. **Route owners** — hand intake, ops, finance, rights, city, growth, or human-gated work to named owners.

## Block Conditions
- evidence does not show why operator involvement matters for the specific site, access, privacy, or commercialization case
- permission, legal, rights/privacy, contract, pricing, or revenue-share judgment is required
- live outreach is requested without approval and recipient-backed contact evidence
- the lane would imply site-operator approval is universally required

## Escalation Conditions
- repeated buyer demand for controlled facility access or operator-approved commercialization
- operator-lane questions expose rights/privacy or revenue-share policy gaps
- a GTM target cannot advance without a human decision on access, permissions, or commercialization
- optional operator work starts displacing the core robot-team buyer lane

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- repeated buyer demand for controlled facility access or operator-approved commercialization
- evidence that privacy or rights boundaries are confusing otherwise qualified buyers
- signs that operators would care about monetization or governance for specific site types
- any request that slips into permission, privacy, legal, contract, or revenue-share judgment
