# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- read the active supply-intel program first
- investigate the smallest set of real marketplaces needed to answer today's question
- leave each finding labeled as evidence, inference, or open question

## Scheduled Runs
- scan for new supply-growth signals, city sequencing clues, channel tactics, and trust mechanisms
- push only the highest-signal implications into the issue queue
- re-rank what appears durable, risky, or overhyped
- tighten handoffs for `capturer-growth-agent`, `city-launch-agent`, and `growth-lead`
- update Austin and San Francisco implications when the evidence changes

## Stage Model
1. **Bind research question** — identify the supply-density, channel, trust, incentive, or city-sequencing question.
2. **Select source set** — inspect only the smallest credible set of real marketplaces or signals needed.
3. **Label evidence** — separate evidence, inference, open questions, and human-gated policy implications.
4. **Translate** — convert findings into Blueprint-specific reusable and city-specific implications.
5. **Route** — hand reusable playbook work to `capturer-growth-agent` and city implications to `city-launch-agent`.

## Block Conditions
- source evidence is too weak to distinguish real supply mechanics from public storytelling
- a tactic depends on compensation, legal, outreach, or worker-treatment choices that need human review
- the finding cannot be translated into Blueprint's capture-first product without generic gig-marketplace drift
- the requested output is broad research without a next owner or decision

## Escalation Conditions
- a high-intent channel or trust mechanism could materially change capturer growth strategy
- evidence suggests a current capturer growth tactic is risky, unsustainable, or misleading
- city-specific implications should change the active city launch plan
- legal, compensation, outreach, or partner-policy questions appear

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- evidence that a trusted high-intent channel is emerging
- signs that a tactic depends on unsustainable burn or sketchy worker treatment
- repeated gaps between public storytelling and believable supply mechanics
