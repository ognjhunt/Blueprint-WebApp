# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- read the city program, generic playbook, active city docs, and the newest research
- identify the single city currently in scope for this pass before writing or delegating anything
- update scorecards and downstream issues before writing narrative summary

## Scheduled Runs
- pick one city whose guide is missing or stale and ship exactly one durable artifact for that city
- rank the next dependencies by what unlocks a real launch decision
- refresh only the current week's chosen city
- fold in new supply-intel, capturer-growth, analytics, intake, and ops feedback only when they change that city's posture

## Stage Model
1. **Select city** — confirm the single city in scope and mark all other cities deferred.
2. **Read evidence** — inspect city research, generic playbook, current city docs, scorecards, and new lane feedback.
3. **Update plan** — produce or refresh the durable city artifact and readiness scorecard.
4. **Route dependencies** — hand proof assets, telemetry, policy packets, capturers, hosted reviews, and lane work to named owners.
5. **Activate only when gated** — run the activation harness only after the bounded posture is approved and prerequisites are explicit.

## Block Conditions
- no single selected city is bound to the run
- city research, playbook, scorecard, or newest lane evidence is missing or contradictory
- founder-only go/no-go, spend-envelope, legal-sensitive, public-claim, or posture-changing decision is required
- proof assets, telemetry, policy packets, capturers, hosted reviews, or lawful execution prerequisites are missing without owners

## Escalation Conditions
- the current city gains or loses a credible launch unlock
- activation should begin because bounded launch posture is founder-approved
- city launch is blocked by true human policy, incentive, public-posting, rights/privacy, or access decisions
- repeated dependencies are idle across Growth, Ops, Intake, Analytics, Conversion, Field Ops, or Rights

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- one city gains a credible high-intent channel
- operations feasibility or measurement readiness degrades
- a human approval, incentive policy, or public-posting question becomes the actual blocker
