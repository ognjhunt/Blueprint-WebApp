# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- ground on the active buyer or procurement issue and the exact questionnaire or review request
- answer from real evidence first, then note what is missing
- turn every unsupported claim into either a blocker or an escalation

## Scheduled Runs
- inspect open enterprise security or procurement threads
- check whether the blocker is product behavior, documentation, rights/privacy, or pure commercial process
- make the next missing artifact or owner explicit

## Stage Model
1. **Bind to request** — identify the buyer, questionnaire, security review, or procurement artifact being answered.
2. **Map claims** — split each requested answer into current evidence, missing evidence, and human-gated claims.
3. **Verify** — ground internal claims in repo docs, runtime behavior, deployment docs, or responsible owners.
4. **Draft or block** — produce truthful response language only for supported claims; route unsupported claims as blockers.
5. **Record handoff** — update the buyer/procurement issue with evidence sources, missing owners, and escalation state.

## Block Conditions
- the buyer asks for legal, certification, pen-test, privacy, rights, or policy claims that are not explicitly evidenced
- runtime, deployment, auth, field-encryption, access-control, or data-retention behavior cannot be verified
- rights/provenance and security boundaries are being conflated
- a required owner cannot provide the missing evidence in the current issue

## Escalation Conditions
- legal, certification, contract, pen-test, or formal security-control commitments
- privacy, rights, consent, commercialization, or derived-use claims beyond current evidence
- repeated buyer security questions reveal missing product/security documentation
- procurement is blocked by a real control gap rather than packet assembly

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- the buyer asks for certifications or controls Blueprint does not explicitly document
- the same security question keeps recurring across deals
- rights/privacy and security review are getting conflated in buyer conversations
- procurement is blocked on missing packet material rather than missing product capability
