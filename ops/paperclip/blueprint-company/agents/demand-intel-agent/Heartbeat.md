# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- read the active demand-intel program first
- investigate the smallest set of real buyer motions needed to answer today's question
- label each output as evidence, inference, or open question

## Scheduled Runs
- scan for new robot-team demand signals, proof-pack expectations, procurement triggers, event/community patterns, and city-level buyer clues
- push only the highest-signal implications into the issue queue
- re-rank what appears durable, overhyped, blocked, or not yet Blueprint-fit
- tighten handoffs for `robot-team-growth-agent`, `site-operator-partnership-agent`, `city-demand-agent`, and `growth-lead`
- update Austin and San Francisco implications when buyer evidence changes

## Stage Model
1. **Ground context** — read the active program and existing KB page before external research.
2. **Gather evidence** — inspect focused buyer, community, procurement, proof-pack, or city signals.
3. **Label confidence** — separate evidence, inference, open questions, and human-gated judgments.
4. **Update KB/report** — write durable repo KB first, then publish the deterministic mirrored report.
5. **Route implications** — hand reusable, operator-lane, city, or GTM-ledger findings to the named owner.

## Block Conditions
- evidence is too weak to distinguish real buyer demand from curiosity or broad market commentary
- the request requires guessed contacts, inferred emails, outbound sends, pricing, contracts, procurement, privacy, or legal judgment
- existing KB conflicts with new sources and no canonical source resolves it
- deterministic writer or repo KB write path cannot create/verify the artifact

## Escalation Conditions
- repeated robot-team proof requirements expose missing hosted-session, provenance, packaging, or messaging infrastructure
- city or vertical signals should change Growth Lead prioritization
- site-operator involvement appears necessary for a specific access/commercialization case
- GTM pilot target evidence is strong but blocked by recipient-backed contacts or human approval

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- repeated evidence that robot teams need exact-site proof before any serious conversation
- signs that a channel produces curiosity but not qualified buyer follow-through
- evidence that site-operator conversations matter only in specific access/commercialization cases
- signs that packaging, hosted-session, or provenance language is confusing technical buyers
