# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- ground on the current issue and timeframe first
- inspect Knowledge, Growth Studio, and Work Queue together before drafting
- keep the digest internal, concise, and action-oriented
- finish with `blueprint-generate-workspace-digest-report`

## Scheduled Runs
- compress the week into highlights, risks, and next actions
- prefer user-visible or operator-visible movement over internal churn
- queue follow-up work only when the digest clearly implies it

## Stage Model
1. **Bind window** — confirm the digest issue, timeframe, target Knowledge surface, and requested scope.
2. **Read workspace truth** — inspect Knowledge, Growth Studio, Work Queue, and relevant Paperclip evidence together.
3. **Draft internally** — summarize what changed, what matters, risks, and next actions without public-marketing language.
4. **Queue follow-up** — create follow-up items only when evidence clearly implies a new owner or action.
5. **Publish proof** — finish through `blueprint-generate-workspace-digest-report` and attach the generated proof.

## Block Conditions
- Knowledge, Growth Studio, Work Queue, or Paperclip evidence is unavailable or contradictory
- the requested digest would require invented claims, unsupported certainty, or external-marketing framing
- follow-up items would duplicate existing tasks without new evidence
- Notion or report-generation paths cannot create and verify the internal artifact

## Escalation Conditions
- workspace evidence reveals a material miss, risk, or unresolved dependency that needs Growth Lead or Chief of Staff routing
- Growth Studio framing and Work Queue reality diverge enough to mislead operators
- repeated digest findings point to Notion hygiene, analytics, or queue-state drift
- a requested digest is actually a public community update or ship broadcast

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- the strongest story is actually a miss, risk, or unresolved dependency
- Growth Studio framing and Work Queue reality diverge
- the digest starts sounding like an external launch draft instead of an internal roundup
