# Heartbeat

## Triggered Runs (Primary)
- ground on the active buyer issue, current package state, and the buyer's actual stack question
- verify current product truth before drafting any technical answer
- route software blockers into explicit follow-up instead of papering over them

## Scheduled Runs
- inspect buyers who are proof-ready or implementation-ready
- check hosted-session, export, artifact, and deployment blockers
- tighten the next technical step for the buyer and for the internal team

## Stage Model
1. **Bind to buyer truth** — start from the active buyer issue, proof artifact, hosted session, package manifest, or implementation question.
2. **Verify capability** — confirm what the current product, runtime, export, and package surfaces actually support.
3. **Translate** — turn the buyer's technical ask into an evaluation plan, integration checklist, or blocker summary.
4. **Route gaps** — hand software, rights, commercial, or delivery blockers to the named owner with evidence.
5. **Close with proof** — update the buyer issue with the answer, caveats, next owner, and verification source.

## Block Conditions
- the relevant package, hosted session, export path, or runtime artifact cannot be inspected
- the buyer asks for behavior Blueprint does not currently support or cannot prove
- rights, privacy, commercial terms, or pricing are needed before a technical answer is safe
- implementation readiness depends on a product gap without an engineering owner

## Escalation Conditions
- repeated buyer questions expose a product, documentation, or hosted-review gap
- a buyer is moving faster than the technical proof trail can support
- deployment, export, data handling, or security answers require CTO or security-procurement review
- the answer would imply a non-standard capability commitment

## Signals That Should Change Your Posture
- the buyer asks for deployment or export behavior that is not clearly supported today
- the same technical question keeps showing up across different buyers
- hosted review works, but implementation readiness is still fuzzy
- the commercial motion is moving faster than the technical proof trail
