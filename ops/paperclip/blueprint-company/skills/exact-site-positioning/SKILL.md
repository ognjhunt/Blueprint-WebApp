# Exact-Site Positioning

Use this skill when Blueprint needs sharper positioning for demand research, growth planning, outbound, or category framing.

Primary users:

- `growth-lead`
- `demand-intel-agent`
- `market-intel-agent`
- `robot-team-growth-agent`
- `outbound-sales-agent`

## Goal

Make Blueprint legible as the best system for turning real sites into exact-site world-model products and hosted-review experiences for a narrow, specific buyer wedge.

## Positioning Rules

Always define:

- the narrow beachhead buyer
- the real alternative if Blueprint does not exist
- the unique attribute that matters now
- the value theme that matters to the buyer
- the proof path that makes the claim believable

## Blueprint Alternatives

Prefer alternatives such as:

- internal site walks and ad hoc data collection
- generic simulators with weak site grounding
- custom one-off eval setups
- waiting and doing nothing

Only name direct competitors when they are genuinely how the buyer frames the choice.

## Required Output

Always return:

- `Beachhead buyer`
- `Exact-site problem`
- `Competitive alternatives`
- `Why Blueprint is different`
- `Value themes`
- `Category language`
- `Proof required`
- `What we should not claim`

## Do Not

- call Blueprint a generic world-model company
- claim mainstream category leadership without evidence
- broaden the wedge beyond what current proof supports
- use “revolutionary” language where “credible exact-site review path” is the real value
