# Founder Decision Packet Standard

Purpose:
- minimize founder review time
- keep founder approvals bounded
- stop vague `Needs Founder` work from reaching Founder OS, Slack, or waiting-on-human queues

Founder-only decision classes:
- city launch / no-launch decisions, including Austin `stay gated` versus `expand` and whether San Francisco starts at all
- new spend envelopes, not routine spend instances inside an already approved envelope
- public claims or posture changes that would change how Blueprint is represented externally
- non-standard pricing, contract, or commercialization commitments outside approved bands or precedent
- rights, privacy, consent, or commercialization exceptions that need company-level judgment
- policy changes that affect trust, legality, or irreversible external commitments

Do not create a founder packet for routine work already owned by named operators inside written guardrails, including:
- invite or access-code issuance inside an approved city or cohort policy
- referral approval inside an approved referral policy
- intake rubric approval
- first-capture activation thresholds
- operator-facing trust kit creation
- analytics deployment validation or reporting-readiness confirmation
- proof-pack quality confirmation
- standard buyer-thread handling and standard quotes inside approved price bands

Hard rules:
- one decision per packet
- one recommendation per packet
- one exact ask per packet
- one named execution owner per packet
- one deadline or next checkpoint per packet
- if any required field is missing, the packet is incomplete and should be routed back before founder review

Required field order:

1. Decision Title
- one-line issue or decision name

2. Why Decision Is Needed Now
- what changed
- what blocks execution if the founder does nothing
- why this cannot wait for the next routine pass

3. Recommended Answer
- the default answer the founder should approve now

4. Alternatives
- realistic alternatives still on the table

5. Downside / Risk
- what could go wrong with the recommended answer

6. Exact Approval Or Info Needed
- the precise permission, credential, answer, or boundary needed from the founder

7. Who Executes Immediately After Approval
- the agent or human owner who moves the work immediately

8. By When
- the execution deadline or next checkpoint

9. Evidence
- the minimum proof supporting the recommendation

10. Non-Scope
- what this decision does not approve

Minimum quality bar:
- no open-ended brainstorming language
- no bundle of unrelated asks
- no recommendation without downside
- no escalation without a named executor
- no founder packet for work that can be decided by Chief of Staff, CTO, Growth Lead, Ops Lead, Finance, or Rights/Privacy

Default fallback when incomplete:
- mark the packet as incomplete
- route it back to Chief of Staff
- do not put it in a founder-facing brief as a loose bullet
