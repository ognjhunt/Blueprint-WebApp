# City Demand Agent - Current Focus

## Priority

Maintain the first two city demand plans for Blueprint's robot-team buyer motion:

1. Austin, TX
2. San Francisco, CA

The job is to convert the reusable robot-team playbook into city operating briefs without rewriting the core product story.

## Product Truth To Preserve

- Blueprint is capture-first and world-model-product-first.
- The primary sellable outputs are site-specific world models, site packages, and hosted sessions on exact sites.
- Hosted evaluation is a managed path on one exact site, not a blanket deployment guarantee.
- Rights, privacy, provenance, and commercialization controls stay attached to the product record.
- Qualification and readiness can support the sale, but they must not become the main story.

## City Planning Scope

- one bounded corridor or wedge per city
- likely robot-team buyer clusters and relevant facility types
- optional site-operator lanes when they matter for access or commercialization
- communities, events, and network surfaces that are useful internally
- proof-pack, hosted-session, intake, and ops conditions that must be true before execution
- readiness scorecards that compare cities without pretending to be exact

## Current Constraints

- No repo-local city-demand skill or program existed at the start of this cycle.
- Austin does not currently have a buyer-facing site-world example in the catalog; current Austin mentions are mock or internal.
- San Francisco demand must be treated as a Bay Area corridor question, not a downtown-SF-only question.
- `ops/paperclip/playbooks/site-operator-access-and-commercialization-playbook.md` is referenced in agent steering but not present in this repo yet, so operator-lane recommendations must stay narrow and clearly marked.
- Public posting, outreach, city-live claims, pricing claims, and guaranteed demand claims remain human-gated.

## Success Criteria

- Austin and San Francisco city playbooks exist in-repo
- each city has a bounded wedge, readiness scorecard, and explicit blockers
- downstream work is issue-ready instead of vague
- the city plans make it obvious what stays generic and what truly localizes
