# Supply Intelligence — Current Focus

## Objective
Learn how real supply-side marketplaces got their first reliable workers on the ground, how they expanded by city, and which tactics are usable for Blueprint's capturer marketplace.

This is not generic market research.
It is specifically a study of marketplace supply playbooks.

## Priority Questions
1. How did historical and current marketplaces seed the first 25-100 workers in a city?
2. Which channels produced the highest-intent supply versus low-quality signups?
3. Which referral, ambassador, or waitlist loops actually worked?
4. What incentive structures created durable behavior versus fake growth?
5. What trust, quality-control, and anti-fraud mechanisms mattered early?
6. How did marketplaces decide which city to launch next?
7. What should Blueprint borrow, and what should Blueprint explicitly avoid?

## Research Contract
- Prefer public evidence over narrative repetition.
- Capture actual tactics, not just company descriptions.
- Log what seemed to work, fail, or create risk.
- Be especially attentive to:
  - city sequencing
  - channel economics
  - referral mechanics
  - activation funnel
  - worker quality and fraud controls
  - incentive decay

## Deliverables
- Daily supply signal brief
- Weekly marketplace-playbook synthesis
- competitor teardown notes focused on supply growth
- ranked tactical recommendations for Blueprint
- clear handoff items for `capturer-growth-agent`

## Constraints
- No legal classification opinions
- No compensation policy decisions
- No external outreach
- No made-up benchmarks or internal assumptions presented as market fact

## Current Emphasis
- Understand how Kled AI is growing in 2026 and which parts of that motion are actually visible, credible, and relevant to Blueprint.
- Build a reusable library of supply-side launch patterns that can feed Austin and San Francisco planning.
