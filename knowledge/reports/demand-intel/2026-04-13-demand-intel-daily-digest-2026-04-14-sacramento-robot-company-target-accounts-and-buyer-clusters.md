# Sacramento Robot Company Target Accounts and Buyer Clusters

## Summary

Sacramento should stay in a narrow proof-led posture. The current target layer is a small cluster of industrial automation and robotics integration buyers, plus one autonomy-corridor signal, that can plausibly evaluate exact-site proof packs.

The practical implication is that Blueprint should lead with exact-site proof, replay, and provenance for industrial buyers first, then keep Cyngn and Waymo as corridor validation rather than trying to turn them into the primary buyer story.

## Evidence

- SIR Robotics publicly presents robotics and automation work tied to production-style workflows, which keeps it in the Sacramento industrial-automation target set.
- VC Automation publicly presents turnkey automation, robotics, PLC, machine vision, and safety integration work, and its CMTC profile places it in West Sacramento.

- Cyngn explicitly ran an autonomous-vehicle roadshow stop in Sacramento aimed at manufacturing and warehousing facilities.
