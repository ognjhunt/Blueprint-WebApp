# Sacramento Robot Company Target Accounts and Buyer Clusters

## Summary

Sacramento demand is concentrated in a small number of robotics-relevant buyer surfaces rather than a broad citywide account pool. The strongest near-term wedge is industrial warehouse and intralogistics, with a second cluster around UC Davis autonomy and robotics research, and a third cluster around ag robotics / field robotics ecosystem anchors in the Sacramento-Woodland orbit.

The practical implication is that Blueprint should lead with exact-site proof packs for warehouse and integrator buyers first, then adapt the same proof motion for autonomy research and ag-robotics channels if the access path is truthful.

## Evidence

- Raymond West is an established material-handling and automation brand with clear regional relevance for Sacramento warehouse automation conversations.
- McClellan Park is actively marketing industrial space, and its public pages list US Cold Storage at the McClellan Park address, which supports the warehouse operator and integrator cluster.
- UC Davis AHMCT publicly describes autonomy, robotics, and construction/maintenance technology research, which makes it a credible regional autonomy research anchor.
- FIRA USA remains an active Sacramento-region ag-robotics event surface, with Woodland/Sacramento-area event materials signaling continuing buyer and community density.

## Inference

- The strongest Sacramento buyer motion is likely to start with exact-site warehouse proof, not with general autonomy branding.
- Local site access and operator introductions matter more than citywide awareness.
- The best proof packs are probably industrial interiors, cross-dock / cold-chain variants, and lab or demo-cell adjacent spaces that mirror warehouse planning workflows.

Why this cluster matters:
- Sacramento-region ag robotics remains an active ecosystem surface.
- Blueprint should treat this as a channel and validation cluster, not as a substitute for exact-site warehouse buyer demand.
