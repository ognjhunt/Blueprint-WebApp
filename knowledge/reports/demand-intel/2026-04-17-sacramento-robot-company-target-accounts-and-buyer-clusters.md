# Sacramento Robot Company Target Accounts and Buyer Clusters

## Summary

Sacramento is still a narrow exact-site proof market. The strongest current buyer layer is a cluster of warehouse automation, robotics integration, and industrial systems accounts, plus a second autonomy-research and ag-robotics validation lane that can plausibly evaluate exact-site proof packs.

The practical implication is that Blueprint should lead with exact-site proof, replay, and provenance for industrial and warehouse buyers first, then keep the autonomy and ag-robotics lane as validation rather than trying to turn it into the primary buyer story.

## Evidence

- SIR Robotics' current public site ties Sacramento to robotic lines and cells for automotive-style processing, handling, finishing, assembly, palletization, and general-industry automation.
- SIR Robotics' corporate office is listed at 8351 Luzon Ave, Sacramento, CA 95828, which keeps the local industrial-automation signal concrete rather than abstract.
- VC Automation's current CMTC profile places it in West Sacramento and says it has seven full-time employees, turnkey robotics-specific automation, UL508A capability, and two Certified Machine Safety Experts on staff.
- VC Automation's homepage says it has certified experts across robot, PLC, and machine-vision platforms and positions the team around automation, integration, safety, and training.
- Axis Robotics' current CMTC profile places it in Orangevale and says it does design, engineering, prototyping, and production of technical test and automation solutions for semiconductor and industrial markets.
- Axis Robotics' CMTC profile also calls out robotics, automation, machine vision, IoT, and software engineering, which keeps it in the technical-buyer lane rather than the generic manufacturing lane.
- Raymond West's Sacramento automation pages explicitly sell inVia Robotics, Locus Robotics, and robotic palletizers, giving the warehouse-automation channel a local, named-buyer surface.
- McClellan Park's Logistics Center II page shows a large Sacramento-region industrial asset with 25 dock positions and close freeway access, which matters as a site-operator and proof-path anchor.
- UC Davis AHMCT publicly describes advanced robotics, sensing, localization, robotics labs, and a realistic rugged-terrain field environment, making it a credible regional autonomy-validation surface.
- FIRA USA explicitly centers autonomous farming and agricultural robotics in Woodland | Sacramento, and its current event materials show live demos, growers, OEMs, and research/community density.
- Waymo still lists Sacramento in its rides network, but that should remain a corridor signal rather than a direct buyer-account signal.

## Recommended Follow-up

- Route SIR Robotics, VC Automation, Axis Robotics, and Raymond West to `robot-team-growth-agent` as the core Sacramento buyer and channel cluster.
- Keep McClellan Park in an operator and access-path lane for warehouse and logistics proof paths.
- Keep UC Davis AHMCT in a technical evaluation lane and FIRA USA in a corridor-validation lane.
- Use Waymo only as local autonomy-corridor validation, not as a primary buyer account.
- Prefer proof-pack language that foregrounds exact-site evaluation, replayable artifacts, and provenance over generic robotics awareness.
