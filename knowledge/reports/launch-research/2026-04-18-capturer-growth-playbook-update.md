# Capturer Growth Playbook Update

## Summary

Blueprint's reusable capturer motion is still a gated, quality-first seeding problem, not a generic signup problem. The playbook now makes the first-25 rule explicit: local, rights-cleared, QA-visible cohorts should come first, and public or semi-public discovery should feed invite or operator-reviewed intake rather than broad open access.
For rights-sensitive exact-site work, the reusable playbook also now points at a smaller hard-capped professional starter cohort, with hardware, insurance, and site-authorization fit treated as approval inputs rather than cleanup after signup.

## Evidence

- The supply-intel sweep shows the same pattern across Kled, Taskrabbit, Uber, Lyft, and DoorDash: local gating, first verified action, and trust controls inside the product loop.
- The capturer supply playbook now states that the first 25 approved capturers should come from gated local cohorts with explicit rights and QA controls.
- The Sacramento capturer prospect package stays draft-first and keeps the roster evidence-bounded until verified local contacts replace source buckets.
- Sacramento's city-opening stack already separates warehouse/facility direct awareness from bounded public-commercial community awareness and keeps both on one tagged intake path.

## Implications

- Keep broad posting as a feeder, not as the first supply layer.
- Keep referral eligibility tied to a first passed capture.
- Keep city widening blocked until first-capture pass rate, repeat readiness, and ops load are visible.
- Keep exact-site or controlled-interior launches on a 5-10 person professional starter cohort until the first proof-ready sites exist.
- Keep the San Diego AEC starter cohort hard-capped at 10 vetted operators until the first approved capturer and proof-ready site are both visible.
- Keep hardware, insurance, and explicit site-authorization proof inside the approval gate when the capture workflow requires them.
- Keep the repo KB as the durable mirror target for any future Notion sync.

## Follow-up

- `conversion-agent`: keep draft recruiting language truthful and specific to the gated first-25 motion.
- `analytics-agent`: keep approval, first-capture, repeat-readiness, and ops-load tracking visible by source.
- `intake-agent`: preserve invite, access-code, and operator-reviewed intake as the default response path.
- `city-launch-agent`: reuse the gated first-25 rule in city launch packets instead of re-inventing local supply logic.
