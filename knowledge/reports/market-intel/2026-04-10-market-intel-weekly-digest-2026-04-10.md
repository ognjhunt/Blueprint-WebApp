# Market Intel Weekly Digest - 2026-04-10

## Summary

Blueprint's market baseline is moving toward cheaper capture, API-shaped world-model products, industrial AI platform packaging, and stricter documentation expectations.

## Evidence

- [7.7] Polycam opened free non-LiDAR iPhone 3D scanning (https://poly.cam/press-release/space-mode-access-expanded-2026): This lowers capture friction and broadens the baseline expectation for simple mobile capture.
- [7.8] World Labs launched a public World API and is framing 3D as code (https://www.worldlabs.ai/blog/announcing-the-world-api): This is a direct platform competitor signal, not just a research update.
- [7.1] NVIDIA expanded Physical AI and digital-twin blueprint packaging (https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-announces-open-physical-ai-data-factory-blueprint-to-accelerate-robotics-vision-ai-agents-and-autonomous-vehicle-development): Robotics and industrial buyers are being trained toward digital-twin-first workflows.
- [6.4] Siemens and NVIDIA expanded manufacturing AI partnership (https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/siemens-and-nvidia-expand-partnership-to-accelerate-ai-capabilities-in-manufacturing): This reinforces the direction that twins, simulation, and orchestration are being sold as one platform stack.
- [7.7] EU and California privacy/compliance pressure is tightening (https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai): Documentation, traceability, and provenance metadata are becoming product inputs rather than back-office artifacts.
- Polycam opened free non-LiDAR iPhone 3D scanning, which widens the floor for capture simplicity and makes good-enough-from-a-phone feel mainstream.
- World Labs launched a public World API and is explicitly positioning 3D as code, which makes it a nearer platform competitor.
- NVIDIA released physical-AI and digital-twin blueprint packaging that pushes market expectations toward platformed integration rather than isolated models.
- Siemens and NVIDIA expanded industrial AI collaboration, reinforcing the buyer expectation that twins, simulation, and AI orchestration belong in one stack.
- CSGaussian: compression and segmentation for 3D Gaussian Splatting are being optimized, which matters for deployable packaging, memory footprint, and downstream editability.
- WorldStereo: controllable video generation and 3D reconstruction are converging through explicit geometric memory, which points at backend options for editable scene capture.
- 3DGS / reconstruction backend shift: the technical center of gravity is moving toward scene capture primitives that can be edited and exported, not just viewed.
