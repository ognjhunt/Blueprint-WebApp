# Market Intel Weekly Digest - 2026-04-17

## Summary

Capture commoditization is accelerating while world-model and robotics platforms harden around delivery, deployment, and documentation

## Evidence

- - [8.8] World Labs Spark 2.0 streamable 3DGS delivery (https://www.worldlabs.ai/blog/spark-2.0): World Labs added LoD, progressive streaming, and virtual-memory style delivery for huge 3DGS worlds on browser-capable devices.
- - [8.6] NVIDIA Physical AI Data Factory Blueprint (https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-announces-open-physical-ai-data-factory-blueprint-to-accelerate-robotics-vision-ai-agents-and-autonomous-vehicle-development): NVIDIA is packaging robotics data generation, curation, synthetic data, RL, and evaluation as a reference architecture.
- - [8.0] Polycam non-LiDAR iPhone capture remains free (https://poly.cam/press-release/space-mode-access-expanded-2026): Polycam continues to widen the capture floor by making spatial capture available on standard iPhones with no LiDAR.
- - [8.2] Agility and Toyota move humanoid deployment into RaaS (https://www.agilityrobotics.com/content/agility-robotics-announces-commercial-agreement-with-toyota-motor-manufacturing-canada): Agility signed a commercial Robots-as-a-Service agreement with Toyota Motor Manufacturing Canada after a successful pilot.
- - [8.1] Figure Helix 02 extends control to the full robot (https://www.figure.ai/news/helix-02): Figure's Helix 02 moves from upper-body control to whole-body walking, manipulation, and balancing as one system.
- - [7.8] EU AI Act energy-consumption consultation adds a new documentation burden (https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/consultations/targeted-consultation-measuring-energy-consumption-and-emissions-ai-models-and-systems): The Commission is now gathering input on energy and emissions reporting for AI models and systems under the AI Act documentation framework.
- Polycam remains the clearest signal that the floor for capture has dropped to standard phones, not specialized hardware.
- World Labs is now spanning both world-model generation and the web rendering stack, which makes it look more like infrastructure than a pure research lab.
- NVIDIA and Siemens keep packaging the industrial AI stack as an operating system, which raises buyer expectations for integration, simulation, and digital-twin defaults.
- Agility's Toyota agreement and Figure's Helix 02 release indicate the humanoid market is still moving toward production language, named pilots, and room-scale autonomy claims.
- 3D Gaussian Splatting is moving toward streaming, semantic, and editability-friendly backends rather than static view synthesis alone.
- GA-GS points at generation-assisted reconstruction for occluded regions, which matters for site capture where dynamic objects block static geometry.
- S2GS shows that semantic splatting can be streamed incrementally without reprocessing the full history, which is the kind of backend improvement that makes large-world delivery more practical.
- The emerging technical center of gravity is not just better reconstruction quality; it is also memory efficiency, streaming, and downstream manipulation.

## Recommended Follow-up

- Keep the capture onboarding path short and explicit, because commodity-device capture is still getting easier.
- Track World Labs Spark 2.0 and World API for export, streaming, and integration patterns that may become standard buyer asks.
- Watch NVIDIA's Physical AI Data Factory Blueprint and the Siemens partnership for the defaults industrial buyers will expect around simulation, synthetic data, and operational digital twins.
- Surface provenance, consent, traceability, and now energy-use metadata explicitly in buyer-facing package contracts and ops docs.
- Treat humanoid deployment announcements as validation of market demand, but keep Blueprint differentiated on site specificity, rights-safe delivery, and buyer workflow usefulness.
