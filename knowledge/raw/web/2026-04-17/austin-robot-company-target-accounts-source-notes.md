## Capture Notes

- Apptronik is Austin-based, spun out of UT Austin, and explicitly sells Apollo into logistics, manufacturing, 3PL, beverage bottling, fulfillment, and consumer packaged goods. That makes it the clearest Austin humanoid anchor and a strong proof-path match for warehouse and production-floor exact-site work.
- Diligent Robotics is Austin-based, rooted in UT Austin research, and positions Moxi around hospitals and care settings. Its public milestones center on hospital pharmacy deliveries and autonomous elevator rides, which makes healthcare a real Austin robotics demand lane rather than a generic brand signal.
- Fox Robotics is Austin-based and sells autonomous forklift trailer loading/unloading for warehouse receiving docks. Its Austin testing-facility demo language maps cleanly to dock workflows and warehouse proof-pack motion.
- ICON is Austin-based and now sells construction-scale robotics both through Titan for builders and through ICON Prime for military and NASA-adjacent infrastructure. That makes construction, national-security infrastructure, and robotics-as-a-system all live Austin buyer surfaces.
- SparkCognition operates HyperWerx, a 50-acre Austin-area proving ground tied to robotics, UAVs, factory automation, oil and gas, and defense events. Its UT Austin partnership adds research legitimacy and makes it an industrial-autonomy and proving-ground anchor.
- Ocean Infinity maintains an Austin office and frames its robotics business around offshore data acquisition, uncrewed vessel operations, and onshore operations centers. That makes Austin relevant as a control and commercial hub for maritime robotics.
- RoyCo Robotics is headquartered in Austin and automates oilfield sand-dump and separator workflows in Midland. It is a narrower energy-automation target, but it is a real Austin robotics company with a clear field-operations use case.
- RobotLAB Austin is an Austin-local robotics integrator, not a product company. It sells deployment, training, and support across restaurants, hotels, assisted living, schools, businesses, and government, so it matters as a channel and buyer-surface interpreter.
- Texas Robotics at UT Austin anchors the research, talent, and community lane. The organization is centered at Anna Hiss Gym on the main UT campus and regularly hosts tours, symposiums, and student-facing robotics programs.
- Austin Robotics & AI is the strongest local community anchor I found. The public site and Meetup page show a large monthly meetup base with engineers, founders, demos, and technical talks, which is useful for channel density but not a direct buyer signal.
