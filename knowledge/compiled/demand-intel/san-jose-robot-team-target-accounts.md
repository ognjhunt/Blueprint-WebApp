# San Jose Robot Team Target Accounts

## Summary

San Jose demand is concentrated in a small set of exact-site robotics surfaces: humanoid deployment, warehouse visibility, agricultural autonomy, semiconductor handling, and robotics integration/community channels.

Blueprint should not lead with generic robotics outreach. It should lead with exact-site proof packs matched to one of four buyer clusters:

1. Humanoid and human-environment robotics
2. Warehouse visibility and inventory automation
3. Agricultural autonomy and field robotics
4. Industrial integration and ecosystem/channel density

## Current State

- Figure is the sharpest humanoid anchor because it is headquartered in San Jose and publicly emphasizes deployment, logistics, and real-world autonomy.
- IntBot is the clearest public-space pilot because SJC is using it as a live testing ground in Terminal B.
- Vimaan is the strongest warehouse visibility anchor because it is based in San Jose and runs a San Jose HQ/demo center for inventory-tracking workflows.
- Bonsai Robotics is the strongest agriculture autonomy anchor because it is headquartered in San Jose and frames its work around field autonomy.
- Kawasaki Robotics is the clearest industrial integration anchor because the company lists a San Jose sales and service office with semiconductor coverage.
- RobotLAB, Silicon Valley Robotics, and Open Robotics are ecosystem and channel anchors rather than direct buyers.

## Evidence

- Figure publicly ties its product to real-world deployment at BMW and says its careers page is centered on its San Jose headquarters.
- Figure's recent technical writeups frame Helix around real-world logistics, long-horizon autonomy, and data collection in human environments.
- IntBot is described in an official San José Mineta International Airport release as a San Jose-based robotics company, and SJC says the robot is being used as a live testing ground.
- Vimaan's contact and demo pages list San Jose HQ locations and describe live warehouse demos for inventory tracking and visibility.
- Vimaan's press pages frame the company around autonomous data collection and inspection in warehouses.
- Bonsai Robotics' official news identifies the company as San Jose-based and positions it around vision-based autonomy for agriculture.
- Kawasaki Robotics' locations page lists a San Jose sales and service office for its semiconductor division.
- RobotLAB's San Jose page emphasizes on-site deployment, training, account management, system integration, and maintenance for local customers.
- Silicon Valley Robotics describes itself as the largest robotics and AI innovation cluster and focuses on commercialization support for the regional robotics ecosystem.
- Open Robotics' contact page lists a San Jose address and positions the organization around ROS, Gazebo, and Open-RMF interoperability infrastructure.

## Inference

- San Jose buyer demand is strongest when Blueprint leads with an exact site, a live workflow, and inspectable proof rather than a generic robotics pitch.
- The city's best targets split cleanly into exact-site buyers and channel/infrastructure anchors.
- Exact-site proof packs for San Jose should fit humanoid workspaces, airport/public-space pilots, warehouse scan workflows, farm autonomy, semiconductor handling, or integration labs.
- Site-only outreach is weaker than proof packs that make replay, export, and provenance obvious.
- Channel and community credibility matters, but only when it shortens the path to a concrete proof review.
- San Jose is a better fit for technical proof-led outbound than for broad awareness campaigns.

- A hosted-review next step that feels like evaluation, not marketing.
