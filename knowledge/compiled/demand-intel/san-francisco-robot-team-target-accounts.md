# San Francisco Robot Team Target Accounts

## Summary

San Francisco demand is concentrated enough to name specific buyer threads without pretending the city is fully activated. The strongest threads are retail store intelligence, humanoid logistics and manipulation, warehouse visibility, enterprise physical AI, drone autonomy for inspection, and the Bay Area commercialization channels that can shorten the path to proof review.

## Current State

- San Francisco city-proper demand is strongest in retail store intelligence and public-facing robotics.
- The broader Bay Area adds humanoid logistics, warehouse visibility, off-road autonomy, truck loading, and drone inspection demand.
- The correct motion is proof-led and workflow-specific, not generic robotics awareness.
- BARA and Silicon Valley Robotics are useful channel accelerators, but they are not substitutes for direct buyer-thread validation.

## Evidence

- Simbe is headquartered in San Francisco and publicly positions Tally as an autonomous inventory robot for retail store intelligence.
- Simbe’s public materials show retail demand in San Francisco and beyond, including Decathlon’s inaugural U.S. store in San Francisco and follow-on deployments at chains such as Schnuck Markets.
- Simbe’s 2026 materials emphasize Tally 4.0, UL 3300 certification, and safe operation in dynamic public retail environments.
- Figure’s careers and company materials place headquarters in San Jose and frame the business around real-world humanoid deployment and general-purpose robotics.
- Figure’s BMW writeup shows an 11-month active deployment on an assembly line with measurable runtime and production contribution.
- Figure’s Helix logistics materials emphasize package manipulation, triaging, and real-world commercial use cases.
- Vimaan lists San Jose headquarters and positions the company around warehouse visibility and warehouse automation.
- Bonsai Robotics is San Jose-based and frames autonomy for off-road environments, fleet management, and agriculture.
- Dexterity is Redwood City-based and says it has production deployment for autonomous truck loading.
- Skydio is headquartered in San Mateo and positions its products for public safety, utilities, national security, asset inspection, and surveying and mapping.
- BARA explicitly connects robotics, capital, and commercialization and runs sessions that connect qualified startups with buyers and investors.
- Silicon Valley Robotics frames itself as a commercialization and events hub for the Bay Area robotics ecosystem.
