# San Diego Robot Team Target Accounts

## Summary

San Diego demand is not evenly distributed. The strongest target surface is a small set of companies and institutions that already speak the language of autonomy, simulation, fleet safety, defense testing, robotics integration, or exact-site operations.

Blueprint should not lead with generic robotics outreach. It should lead with exact-site proof packs matched to one of four buyer clusters:

1. Warehouse and retail autonomy
2. Defense autonomy and unmanned systems
3. Industrial automation and systems integration
4. Research, talent, and channel anchors

## Current State

- Brain Corp is the cleanest warehouse and retail autonomy fit in San Diego.
- Shield AI and GA-ASI are the strongest defense autonomy anchors.
- UC San Diego Contextual Robotics Institute is the strongest research and legitimacy anchor.
- San Diego Tech Hub is a useful channel surface, not a buyer proof pack.

## Evidence

- Brain Corp publicly sells autonomy software for inventory scanning, floor care, and remote site management, and it has a San Diego development-lab history.
- Shield AI publicly frames itself as a San Diego autonomy company building defense aircraft and mission autonomy software, with live-flight and simulation language that maps closely to Blueprint's proof-pack and hosted-review motions.
- GA-ASI publishes San Diego-labeled autonomy demos around the MQ-20 Avenger and positions the platform as a test bed for autonomous UAS development.
- UC San Diego's Contextual Robotics Institute explicitly positions San Diego as a robotics hub and organizes industry-facing robotics forums and autonomous-systems work.
- San Diego Tech Hub is not a robot buyer account, but it is a credible relationship channel for introductions and talent.

## Inference

- The best exact-site proof packs for San Diego are likely warehouses, hangars, integration labs, depots, marine facilities, and robotics demo cells.
- Exact-site proof matters more than office-address proximity. A company can be San Diego-relevant without being a fit if the site type does not match the workflow they actually buy.
- The strongest cluster split is defense autonomy, warehouse autonomy, industrial integration, and local ecosystem influence.
