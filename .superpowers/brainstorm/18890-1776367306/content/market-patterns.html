SKU clarity

The offer reads clearly when each card maps to a distinct operational mode, access level, or output bundle.

What that implies for Blueprint’s three cards

1

Buy the site package

Illustration, not photoreal hero. Show the data bundle itself: walkthrough frames, pose/timestamp track, depth/geometry layer, manifest, provenance and rights packet. The message is “you get the ingredients to build and host your own site-specific world model.”

2

Run a hosted evaluation

Most photoreal card. Show the exact-site runtime and rerun/export loop, because this is the closest thing to a rented operational surface, similar to compute/runtime access.

3

Plan a custom program

Controlled bespoke illustration. Show a private capture + managed model/data engagement around one buyer and one facility, with exclusivity and human-guided scope as the core signal.

Recommended final visual system

Recommended

  • Card 1: structured data / bundle illustration
  • Card 2: photoreal hosted-runtime preview
  • Card 3: premium exclusivity / custom-program illustration

Why not 3 photoreal cards?

  • “Package” and “exclusivity” are not naturally photoreal objects
  • Photoreal scenes can blur the difference between data, runtime, and bespoke engagement
  • The cards get clearer when the medium matches the SKU
