Which photorealistic direction should drive these three offer thumbnails?

All three options stay grounded in Blueprint's real-site capture, hosted runtime, and bespoke engagement model. I recommend B.

A. Facility-Only Realism
Real warehouse / service corridor / industrial room

Each card looks like a high-end still from a real site, with minimal product framing.

Pure capture realism

Most grounded and least “software-y.” Strong for trust, weaker at showing what the buyer actually receives.

B. Productized Deliverable Scenes
Site view / walkthrough / 3DGS
Workspace / package / runtime / private program

Each card shows a real-site scene plus the specific product layer being sold around it.

Recommended: what you buy, shown photorealistically

Best fit for these cards. Feels real, premium, and commercial without drifting into abstract SaaS art.

Boardroom + monitors + protected assets + premium lighting

Leans upscale and bespoke, especially for the custom program card.

Enterprise exclusivity

Feels expensive and managed, but risks underselling the exact-site capture provenance for the first two cards.
